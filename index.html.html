Building Momentum Together

Since October 2025, the Luseland & District Chamber of Commerce has entered a new stage of growth and community involvement.

With the introduction of the Economic Development Officer role, the Chamber has been able to organize its work more effectively, move projects forward faster, strengthen communication with local businesses and partners, and begin turning long-standing ideas into active initiatives.

Today, the Chamber is not only an organization. It is becoming a practical tool for community development, business support, and regional collaboration.

Strategic Priority

Highway 31: From Idea to Action

One of the Chamber's most important strategic priorities has been the Highway 31 initiative. Over the past several months, the conversation has moved from "Should something be done?" to "How do we move this forward together?"

The Chamber was among the first to raise the issue of how the condition of Highway 31 affects local and regional life, including business logistics, road safety, tourism, investment, and the overall attractiveness of the area.

What began as a local concern has now grown into a broader regional conversation, with neighbouring municipalities recognizing Highway 31 as a shared challenge and confirming a willingness to collaborate.

Regional SupportMunicipalities and RMs across the region have expressed support and are actively engaging in a coordinated effort to move this initiative forward.
Professional PlanningThe proposed initiative would focus on technical assessment and measurable data.
Future FundingA stronger, data-based case can support future repair and improvement funding.

Moving Toward a Professional Infrastructure Project

The next step is the development of the Highway 31 Asset Management Initiative for the Kerrobert-Macklin corridor.

What It Would Include

  • technical road condition assessment
  • accurate and measurable data collection
  • a long-term repair and improvement plan
  • a stronger case for future funding

What Is Needed

  • participation from at least five municipalities
  • initial shared seed funding
  • regional cooperation and practical next steps
  • continued business and community support

Data, Safety & Better Planning

The Chamber has also supported the Town's initiative to install traffic monitoring equipment. A letter of support was submitted to SGI, highlighting the importance of accurate traffic data, identifying high-risk areas, and planning infrastructure improvements based on real numbers.

This is part of a larger shift from reacting to problems after they happen to planning ahead with reliable information.

For Business

Better data can support improved logistics, safer transportation, stronger regional connections, and future economic growth.

For Community

Reliable traffic information helps identify risks, strengthen safety planning, and support decisions that reflect real local conditions.

Community & Business Engagement

Alongside long-term strategic projects, the Chamber has also focused on being present in the everyday life of the community.

Community Events

Participation in Santa Claus Day and the Treasure Hunt, in collaboration with the Luseland Development Committee, brought strong family engagement and helped increase visibility for local businesses.

Safety Presentations

The Chamber supported a Driving Safety Presentation for Grades 9-12 and a Business Safety Presentation focused on practical tools and real-life small-town business scenarios.

Wellness Workshop

A Health, Fitness & Nutrition workshop, delivered by a local expert, focused on wellness, productivity, and daily life.

Workforce Workshop

A workshop on Hiring Foreign Workers through the LMIA process addressed real workforce challenges with practical, case-based guidance.

Welcome Package: More Than a Brochure

Significant work has been completed on the Luseland Welcome Package, including collecting local business information, preparing materials about the community and its history, developing the structure of the package, and identifying the best ways to share it.

The Welcome Package is a tool to help new residents feel at home, a platform to showcase local businesses, and a way to tell Luseland's story with pride.

  • planned distribution through the Town Office
  • planned availability at the local motel
  • online access through the website

Tourism Packages: Building Local Experiences

Work has begun on developing tourism packages for Luseland. The idea is to bring local businesses together into ready-to-book experiences that may include accommodation, food, activities, local history, wellness, or photography.

Possible Experiences

  • museum and dining experience
  • country school tour
  • wellness experiences
  • photography sessions

Why It Matters

This approach can create new customer traffic, additional revenue, and more collaboration between local businesses.
